Branches

Sharks Inc. has eight contributing branches, each providing unique products and transport solutions. Between our departments, we work with over five hundred wholesale partners to equitably and sustainably source an incredibly diverse range of products at an affordable price.

Cow and Frilled Sharks

Hexanchiformes Branch

One of the earliest branches at Sharks Inc., Cow and Frilled Sharks hold five of our oldest continuing partners.

Gulper, Kite-Fin, Bramble, Lantern, Rough, Sleeper, and Dogfish Sharks

Squaliformes Branch

One of our largest branches, the Squaliformes Branch holds approximately 126 partners and wholesale contributors. Many of these partners possess photophores, luminous organs, and exhibit intrinsic bioluminescence.

Sawsharks

Pristiophoriformes Branch

The Pristiophoriform Branch has ten partners and wholesale contributors. These partners are notable for a long snout edged with sharp teeth, used to source products.

Angelsharks

Squatiniformes Branch

The 26 partners within this branch are unique for their flattened bodies and broad pectoral fins, giving them a strong resemblance to rays. Due to substantial fishing pressure from competitors, many of our partners in this branch are considered critically endangered.

Bullhead Shark

Heterodontiformes Branch

Following company downsizing, there is currently only a single family, and ten wholesale contributors in this branch.

Mackerel Sharks

Lamniformes Branch

This branch is home to some of our most famous partners, including the great white and mako sharks. It holds some of our physically largest continuing partners, including the largest macropredatory shark ever, and large planktivores. Unfortunately, following department downsizing over the last 20 million years, fifteen of the original 290+ contributors remain.

Carpet Sharks

Orectolobiformes Branch

With roughly 43 wholesale contributors, the Orectolobiformes Branch contains a diverse range of appearances, sizes, and product types. This branch also includes the largest wholesale contributor—the whaleshark—which is notably the largest fish to date.

Ground Sharks

Carcharhiniformes Branch

Shark Inc.'s largest branch holds over 270 wholesale contributors, including the popular catsharks, swell sharks, and hound sharks.
